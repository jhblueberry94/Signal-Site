The product

How Signal Works

How can a daily question help fix failing LinkedIn content?

A daily, research-backed question removes the blank page problem by giving a specific prompt to react to, rather than asking someone to invent a topic from nothing. Answering a real question tends to surface an actual opinion, which is what gives a post a point of view worth engaging with.

Is Signal a direct replacement for Supergrow?

Not exactly, because the two tools solve different problems. Supergrow is a content production tool: templates, AI drafts, scheduling.

Signal is a thinking tool: it gives you one research-backed question a day across three content pillars, and turns your answer into a structured post using the Story Arc framework. Professionals who switch from Supergrow to Signal are usually doing so because they want help with ideation, not just drafting.

Does Signal draft posts for you?

Signal gives you a research-backed question each morning, you write your answer in your own words, and Signal then structures that answer into a LinkedIn post using the Story Arc framework: Hook, Tension, Turn, Insight, Landing. The drafting draws directly from what you wrote, so the thinking and the voice remain yours throughout.

What is the Story Arc framework Signal uses?

The Story Arc framework used by Signal structures LinkedIn posts across five stages: Hook (the opening line that stops the scroll), Tension (the problem or challenge the post addresses), Turn (the shift in perspective or the lesson learned), Insight (the specific takeaway the reader can apply), and Landing (the closing line that leaves a clear impression). This structure is drawn from narrative theory and is designed to hold attention across the full post rather than front-loading everything into the first line.

LinkedIn strategy

Content Strategy

Why does my LinkedIn content get no engagement even when I post consistently?

Consistent posting without a clear point of view rarely builds engagement, because the content reads as generic. If your posts could be published by any other professional in your field without changing a word, readers have no reason to comment, save or share.

The fix is having something specific to say before you write, not posting more often.

Will better hooks and formatting fix low engagement on LinkedIn?

Better hooks and formatting can improve how a post is read, but they cannot fix a post that has nothing distinctive to say. Formatting affects whether people stop scrolling for the first line.

It does not affect whether they comment, save or follow, which depends on whether the content has a real point of view.

Why do generic LinkedIn posts perform worse on the algorithm?

LinkedIn's algorithm prioritises posts that generate meaningful interaction such as comments and dwell time, not just impressions. Generic content that reads like advice anyone could give tends to get passive reactions, such as likes, but few comments, which signals low relevance and limits future reach.

What is the real reason most B2B professionals struggle with LinkedIn content?

Most B2B professionals struggle with LinkedIn not because they can't write, but because they don't know what to say. Topic ideation, deciding what's worth posting about today, is the actual bottleneck.

Writing skill becomes irrelevant if the underlying idea is generic.

What is the best time to post on LinkedIn in 2026?

Tuesday to Thursday mornings, roughly 8am to 11am in your audience's time zone, remain the most reliable window for B2B engagement on LinkedIn. But the gap between a 'good' and 'best' time slot is small, and LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm rewards consistency and dwell time far more than the exact hour a post goes live.

Does posting time still matter on LinkedIn in 2026?

Posting time still matters, but less than it did before LinkedIn's algorithm updates extended the evaluation window for posts. A post can now gain traction hours or even a day after publishing if it earns meaningful engagement, which reduces the penalty for posting outside peak hours.

Is it better to post consistently or to optimise for the perfect time?

Consistency beats precision timing for almost every B2B professional on LinkedIn. A reasonable posting time maintained three times a week for six months will outperform a perfectly timed post published once a month, because the algorithm and the audience both reward accounts that show up reliably.

Why do I struggle to post on LinkedIn even when I know the best time?

Most B2B professionals who research posting times are actually stuck on a different problem: they don't know what to write about. Knowing the optimal hour doesn't help if the post itself doesn't exist, which is why topic ideation, not scheduling, is usually the real blocker.

How long does it take to get consulting clients from LinkedIn?

Most consultants who post consistently start seeing inbound interest within two to three months, but the first paid engagement often takes longer because buyers need repeated exposure before they trust a stranger with their business. Treat the first 90 days as trust-building, not lead generation.

Do I need to post every day to get consulting clients on LinkedIn?

No. Three posts a week, sustained for months without long gaps, builds more trust than daily posting that burns out after three weeks.

Consistency over time matters more than frequency in any given week, especially for independent consultants juggling delivery work.

Should I send cold DMs to get consulting clients on LinkedIn?

Cold DMs work better once a prospect has already seen your posts and formed an opinion of how you think. Sent without that context, most cold outreach reads as generic and gets ignored.

Content does the trust-building work that makes outreach worth sending.

What should consultants avoid posting on LinkedIn?

Avoid generic listicle-style tips ('5 ways to scale your business'), reposted quote graphics, and results-only posts that read as bragging without context. None of these demonstrate how you actually think, which is what prospective clients are evaluating.

How do I stop running out of things to post on LinkedIn?

Running out of things to post is almost always an ideation problem, not a writing problem. The fix is to have a structured source of topics rather than relying on inspiration to strike.

For consultants, that means drawing from three content pillars: what's happening in your market, your professional journey and hard-won experience, and the specific work you do for clients. Signal automates this by surfacing one research-backed question from each pillar every morning, so you are never starting from a blank page.

How often should I post on LinkedIn in 2026?

Three to four times per week is the optimal posting cadence for most B2B professionals in 2026. Consistency matters more than frequency.

Posting every day with average content performs worse than posting three times a week with content that reflects genuine expertise.

What type of content performs best on LinkedIn for B2B professionals?

In 2026, text-based posts that share a specific perspective, lesson, or insight consistently outperform most other formats for B2B professionals. Short-form video and carousels also perform well, but they require significantly more production time.

The format that performs best is the one you can produce consistently with content that reflects genuine expertise.

How long does it take to see results from a LinkedIn content strategy?

Most B2B professionals see meaningful engagement growth within 90 days of consistent posting. Pipeline impact typically takes three to six months.

LinkedIn rewards consistency and topical depth over time, so the compounding effect builds gradually rather than appearing all at once.

What is the LinkedIn algorithm looking for in 2026?

The LinkedIn algorithm in 2026 prioritises content that generates meaningful engagement from relevant professional audiences. Specifically: dwell time (how long people spend reading), early engagement rate in the first 60 to 90 minutes after posting, comment quality, and whether the people engaging are professionally relevant to the topic.

How often should consultants post on LinkedIn?

Three times a week is a realistic and sustainable target for most independent consultants. Consistency matters more than volume.

One well-observed post per day from a topic you genuinely know outperforms five generic posts.

What makes a LinkedIn post attract consulting clients rather than just followers?

Posts that demonstrate how you think, not just what you know. Clients hire consultants for judgement.

Posts that show a decision made, a problem reframed, or a counterintuitive observation give potential clients evidence of that judgement before they ever speak to you.

Should consultants use AI to write their LinkedIn posts?

AI is most useful for structure and editing, not for generating the thinking. A post written entirely by AI from a generic prompt will not sound like you and will not demonstrate the specific expertise that attracts clients.

The most effective approach is to use AI to shape and format your own genuine observations, which is how Signal is designed to work.

How often should leadership advisors and trainers post on LinkedIn?

Three times a week is the practical benchmark for leadership advisors and trainers. That's frequent enough to stay visible between facilitation days and client engagements, but sustainable alongside delivery work.

Consistency over months matters more than frequency in any single week.

What should leadership advisors post about on LinkedIn?

The strongest content for leadership advisors and trainers comes from the moments inside workshops, coaching sessions and offsites where something shifted: a question that changed the room, a model that finally landed, a pattern noticed across multiple client organisations. This is more effective than generic leadership tips because it demonstrates the methodology rather than just naming it.

Why do generic leadership tips perform worse on LinkedIn in 2026?

LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm update reduces reach for content that reads as templated or AI-generated, and generic leadership tips (lists like '5 traits of great leaders') are exactly the pattern it suppresses, because thousands of accounts post near-identical versions. Specific, first-person accounts of real client work perform better both with readers and with the algorithm.

How is LinkedIn different for leadership advisors compared with consultants?

Consultants are usually hired to solve a defined business problem, while leadership advisors and trainers are hired to change how people think, decide and behave, which is harder to demonstrate in a portfolio. LinkedIn content for this audience needs to make the methodology itself visible, not just the outcomes, because buyers are evaluating the advisor's way of thinking as much as their track record.

Tools & alternatives

Tools & Comparisons

What is the best Supergrow alternative for B2B professionals?

It depends on what's actually broken in your LinkedIn workflow. If the problem is template-sounding output, AuthoredUp or Taplio's editor features can help with formatting and polish.

If the problem is not knowing what to post in the first place, which is the more common issue for consultants, fractional executives and agency owners, Signal is built specifically to solve that by giving you a research-backed question every morning instead of another template library.

Why are people looking for alternatives to Supergrow?

The most common complaint is that Supergrow's AI-generated drafts and templates produce content that reads as generic, regardless of the prompt used. Supergrow is built around AI writing and template libraries, which speeds up drafting but does not address the underlying problem most B2B professionals have: not knowing what to write about in the first place.

Does Supergrow work for consultants and fractional executives?

Supergrow's templates are built for a broad market and are not written with consultants, fractional executives, agency owners or executive coaches specifically in mind. Professionals in these roles often find the output needs heavy editing to sound like them, and the underlying ideation problem, knowing what to say each day, remains unsolved.

What is the best LinkedIn content tool for consultants in 2026?

For consultants, Signal is the strongest choice in 2026 because it solves the ideation problem rather than just the drafting problem. It generates research-backed questions from what your market is actively discussing, so you always have something relevant and specific to say.

AuthoredUp is a strong secondary tool for tracking what content is performing and repurposing it.

Is Taplio worth it for B2B professionals in 2026?

Taplio is worth it for B2B sales teams and high-volume LinkedIn creators who need scheduling, CRM integration, and a viral post library. For independent consultants, fractional executives, and coaches building a personal brand, the features that justify the price at higher tiers are largely irrelevant.

Signal, AuthoredUp, or Supergrow will typically serve this audience better at lower cost.

Can I use multiple LinkedIn content tools together?

Yes, and many professionals do. A common combination is Signal for ideation and drafting, AuthoredUp for tracking performance and repurposing, and a separate scheduling tool for posting at optimal times.

The tools serve different functions in the content workflow, so combining them is not redundant.

What should I look for in a LinkedIn content tool in 2026?

In 2026, the most important question is whether the tool solves the ideation problem or only the drafting problem. Tools that only help you write faster do not solve the harder challenge of knowing what to say.

Look for tools that help you identify what your specific audience is interested in, produce output that sounds like you rather than like a template, and fit into a workflow you can sustain week after week.

What is the best LinkedIn tool for independent consultants?

The best LinkedIn tool for independent consultants depends on where the workflow actually breaks down. If the problem is not knowing what to post about each day, which is the most common blocker for consultants, Signal is the strongest fit: it surfaces one research-backed question every morning and structures your answer into a post.

If you already have ideas but want better formatting and post analytics, AuthoredUp handles that job without adding an AI writing layer on top.

Do I need a LinkedIn content tool if I only post twice a week?

Yes, and arguably more so. Posting twice a week sounds manageable until you add up the time spent deciding what to say before you write a single word.

Research from the Content Marketing Institute (2024) found that topic ideation is the single biggest time cost in content creation for B2B professionals, ahead of writing and editing. A tool that solves ideation pays for itself even at two posts per week, because it removes the friction that causes most consultants to stop posting altogether.

What is the difference between a LinkedIn scheduling tool and a content tool?

A LinkedIn scheduling tool handles when your post goes live. A content tool helps you decide what to say, structure it well, and publish consistently.

Most platforms sold as content tools are actually scheduling tools with an AI drafting layer on top. True content tools address the ideation stage, not just the drafting and publishing stages.

For consultants, the scheduling function is rarely the bottleneck. The bottleneck is the blank page.

Is Taplio worth it for consultants?

Taplio is worth considering if you post at high frequency and need scheduling, team management, and performance analytics across multiple accounts. For most independent consultants posting two to four times per week, the operational features Taplio is built around are more than you need, and the inspiration feed it relies on for ideation tends to encourage borrowing formats from other creators rather than developing your own point of view.

Is Scripe good for solo professionals or just teams?

Scripe's collaboration and team content features are strongest for coordinated teams. Solo professionals can still use the voice-to-post mechanic, but the pricing and feature set are optimised for groups rather than individuals posting once or twice a week.

Scripe assumes you know what to say; Signal helps you figure it out.

The two tools solve different problems.
